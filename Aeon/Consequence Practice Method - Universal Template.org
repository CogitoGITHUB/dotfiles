* Overview
This template follows the **Consequence Practice Method**:
- Define actions/practices
- Predict consequences
- Execute
- Observe actual consequences
- Reflect and iterate

It is universal: you can apply it to skills, experiments, learning, habits, or any system.

* Instructions
1. Fill in the **Area of Practice**.
2. List the **Actions** you want to test.
3. Predict expected consequences.
4. Execute and log real outcomes.

* Metrics & Analysis
#+BEGIN_SRC emacs-lisp
;; Example: simple scoring of outcome vs predicted
(defun consequence-score (predicted actual)
  "Score consequences: 1 = fully as predicted, 0 = not at all, 0.5 = partial match"
  (cond ((equal predicted actual) 1)
        ((string-match-p (regexp-quote predicted) actual) 0.5)
        (t 0)))
#+END_SRC
